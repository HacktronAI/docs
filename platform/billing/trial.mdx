title: "Trial & activation"
description: "How the 14-day Dev-seat trial works, what it includes, and when to skip it."
---

<Info>
  **Owner-only.** Only an organization Owner can start the trial, add the first payment method, or activate paid billing. Admins and Members can view billing details but cannot change them.
</Info>

Hacktron offers a **14-day Dev-seat trial** for organizations that have never trialed before. The trial is one-time per organization and starts the moment an Owner adds a payment method on the **Billing** page.

This page covers how the trial behaves, what changes when it ends, and the path for organizations that prefer to skip it.

## At a glance

| Property | Trial behavior |
| --- | --- |
| **Duration** | 14 days from activation (some orgs have a different negotiated duration) |
| **Payment method** | Required to start. No charge during the trial. |
| **Seat assignment** | Auto-approval — Dev seats assign as PRs arrive |
| **Default seat cap** | 10 seats |
| **Per-seat PR cap** | Default 50 PRs per billing period (configurable) |
| **Spillover** | Not available during trial |
| **One-time** | Each organization gets at most **one** trial, ever |

## How the trial starts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect a SCM provider">
    GitHub, GitLab, or Bitbucket. See [Quickstart](/platform/quickstart) for the connection flow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="An Owner opens Billing">
    The Owner navigates to **Billing** in the Platform.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a payment method">
    Card details go straight to Stripe — Hacktron never sees raw card data. Adding a payment method is required to start the trial.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the trial">
    The Owner confirms the trial. The organization enters the 14-day window immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

During the trial, Dev seats auto-assign as PRs come in. There is no hard cap on PRs at the organization level — each seat is metered against the per-seat PR cap (50 PRs per billing period by default).

## What happens when the trial ends

The trial ends one of three ways:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Trial completes (paid plan)">
    On day 14, the trial transitions to a paid plan automatically. Stripe issues the first invoice; the **peak seat count** during the trial is **reset**, so the first paid invoice bills only for seats currently active — not the trial peak.

    [See Peak billing](/platform/billing/seats) for the HWM mechanics.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Owner ends trial early">
    The Owner can end the trial at any time from the Billing page. This converts the organization to a paid plan immediately, with the same HWM reset behavior as natural expiry.

    Use this if the team is ready to commit before day 14 and wants the period boundary to start now.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Owner cancels during trial">
    Canceling during the trial sets `cancel_at_period_end = true`. The trial continues to run; when day 14 arrives, the subscription is canceled instead of converted. No charges are issued.

    The Owner can reactivate any time before day 14 with one click.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## The trial-skip path

<Note>
  This is the path for organizations that already know they want a paid plan and prefer to start the billing period today rather than 14 days from now.
</Note>

Some teams want to go straight to paid — for example, when migrating from another vendor mid-quarter. To skip the trial:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Owner adds a payment method on Billing">
    Same as starting the trial.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Owner chooses 'Start paid plan' instead of 'Start trial'">
    The Billing page surfaces both options when no subscription exists yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Subscription activates immediately">
    Seats auto-assign as PRs arrive. The billing period starts today. The first invoice issues at the period close.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  **The trial is one-time per organization.** Once a trial has been started — even if it was canceled, expired without conversion, or converted and then canceled — that organization can never trial again. This applies to both natural-trial and skip-trial paths: starting the trial flips a permanent flag on the organization. If your organization has already used its trial and you cancel later, your only path back to paid is **Resubscribe**, not a new trial.
</Warning>

## What's not available during the trial

- **Spillover billing.** Spillover requires an `ACTIVE` paid subscription — see [Spillover](/platform/billing/spillover). PRs past the per-seat cap during a trial are hard-blocked, not billed as overage.
- **Pentest credit purchases unrelated to seats.** These are independent — you can buy pentest credits without an active subscription, but seat-level features stay gated by trial/paid status.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The Owner clicks 'Start trial' but nothing happens.">
    Check that a payment method has been attached. Trial activation requires a payment method on file even though no charge is issued.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="We trialed last quarter and canceled. Can we trial again?">
    No. Each organization is permitted one trial only. If the org has used its trial, the Billing page will show **Resubscribe** instead of **Start trial**. There is no admin override.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens to seats assigned during the trial when we convert to paid?">
    They stay assigned. The peak seat count is reset on conversion so the first paid invoice bills only for currently-active seats, not the trial peak. See [Peak billing](/platform/billing/seats).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can we shorten or extend the trial?">
    Trials default to 14 days. If your organization has a negotiated different duration, the Billing page will reflect it. Contact your Hacktron point of contact to negotiate; the default cannot be self-served.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
