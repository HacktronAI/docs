## Prerequisites

First, make sure that your organization has an active Slack integration.

<Card
  title="Set up Slack for your organization"

>
  Connect Slack and choose the notification channel.

## Manage findings from Slack

When Hacktron's Slack app sends a finding in your configured channel,
you can leave a triage comment and mark the finding as a false positive or accepted risk,
or mark the finding as valid.

Open the finding card and click on **Mark Valid** or **Triage Comment**.

<img src="/images/slack_comment.png" alt="Triage comments in Slack" />

## Link your Slack identity

To perform actions on findings directly from Slack and for Hacktron to tag
you on Slack, you need to link your Slack identity to your Hacktron account
so that Hacktron can recognize you on Slack.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Account page">
    Click on your organization name in the top left corner and go to
    **Account**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect Slack">
    Scroll down to **Connected Accounts** and click on **Connect** next to
    **Slack**.

    <img src="/images/connected_accounts.png" alt="Connected Accounts" />

  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize Hacktron">
    Sign in to Slack and approve the requested access.

    <Tip>
      If you are signed in to multiple Slack workspaces, make sure you select the right workspace.
      The workspace must be the same as the one configured in the [organization-wide Slack integration](/platform/communication-apps/slack).
    </Tip>

    <img src="/images/authorize_slack.png" alt="Slack authorization" />

    Click on **Accept and continue**.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to Hacktron">
    After authorization, Hacktron redirects you back to **Account**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can now provide feedback and act on findings directly from Slack.
